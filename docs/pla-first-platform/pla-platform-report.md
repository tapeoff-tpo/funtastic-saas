# Project FUN-TASTIC - PLA First Modular Platform Report

Checked: 2026-07-18

## Executive Summary

If FUN-TASTIC starts a platform business using only PLA, the first market should be:

> **A modular desktop micro-organization platform for home-office, stationery and creator desks.**

The MVP should be sold as a **Starter Kit**, not as an empty Base. The first purchase must solve a complete desk-organization job before extensibility becomes valuable.

PLA is sufficient because the selected market is indoors, near room temperature, low-load, dry, visually sensitive and dominated by small accessories. It is not sufficient for the entire future platform. The operating boundary must exclude hot surfaces, vehicles, outdoors, continuous water, large shelves, high loads and impact-heavy parts.

**Final verdict: GO WITH LIMITATIONS.**

The decision authorizes market validation and product-requirement definition. It does not authorize CAD or finalize a connector, base geometry or dimension.

## 1. Evidence Used

This phase extends the Phase 3 platform study with PLA-specific market evidence:

- A MakerWorld modular desk organizer provides 5 tray sizes and more than 20 swappable blocks; comments mention printing several modules and adapting them to individual needs.
- Etsy's 3D-print tool-organizer category displayed more than 5,000 items, including a modular desktop organizer listing with approximately 1,400 reviews.
- Gaming controller/headphone categories contain hundreds to thousands of products and listings with hundreds or thousands of reviews.
- Figure-display categories show modular and expandable systems with hundreds of reviews.
- Camera battery organizers show paid demand for model-specific holders; a Gridfinity Sony battery holder records positive fit comments.
- MakerWorld printer-tool collections show large download counts for clips, tool sets and modular printer accessories.
- Coffee organizers have strong demand, but many use cases sit near heat, liquid or food-contact boundaries.

Marketplace result counts are demand signals, not market share or sales volume. No sales quantity was invented.

## 2. PLA Material Boundary

Bambu describes PLA Basic as easy to print, smooth-finished and available in up to 30 colors. Its published comparison lists PLA Basic heat-deflection temperature around 57°C at 0.45 MPa. Polymaker's regular PLA data reports approximately 58-60°C HDT and also shows weaker Z-direction strength than XY-direction strength.

Therefore, the platform boundary is:

### Allowed for the MVP

- Indoor room-temperature use
- Low static load
- Small, replaceable accessories
- Dry desk, vanity and display surfaces
- Color- and finish-led products
- Parts whose failure cannot injure a person or damage valuable equipment

### Excluded from the MVP

- Cars, direct sunlight and outdoor use
- Hot coffee-machine surfaces and heated appliances
- Continuous water exposure or shower interiors
- Large shelves, monitor/laptop load-bearing structures and heavy tools
- Repeated bending, impact or high-retention clips without physical validation
- Food-contact claims

## 3. Why PETG Is Not Required for the First Platform

PETG would widen the environmental and impact boundary, but it is not necessary to prove the first business hypothesis.

1. The selected customer problem is organization, visibility and personalization, not heat or impact resistance.
2. The intended modules hold pens, cards, cables, phones, small tools and lightweight headphones at indoor temperatures.
3. PLA's stiffness is useful for trays, dividers, rests and display parts that should retain their shape.
4. Commercial and community examples already use PLA for desk and modular storage products.
5. Restricting one material reduces profile variation, color/material inventory and production learning during MVP validation.

This is a scope decision, not a claim that PLA is universally durable. Physical load, creep and drop testing remain required before sale.

## 4. Why PLA Can Be a Competitive Advantage

### Production advantage

- Bambu's default-oriented PLA proposition emphasizes reliable printing and smooth finish.
- Low warping and broad printer familiarity support small-batch repeatability.
- One material simplifies scheduling, inspection standards and failed-print diagnosis.

### Customer advantage

- A broad color palette supports desk themes, identity, gifts and later collection expansion.
- Small-batch FDM can serve object-specific long-tail modules that injection molding cannot economically stock.
- Personal names, labels and coordinated color sets can be produced without new tooling.
- Small modules are inexpensive to ship and replace compared with large bases or shelves.

### Platform advantage

- Digital inventory can be broader than physical inventory.
- Modules can be introduced, measured and retired without committing to molds.
- A successful Base can support cross-category migration from desk to stationery, gaming, camera and hobby uses.

## 6. Why Desk Micro-organization Ranks First

Desk organization is the most practical launch territory because it combines:

- Broad customer reach across work, study, gaming and hobbies
- Many small, low-load objects with repeated organization needs
- Strong color and personalization value
- Low shipping volume for follow-up modules
- Natural adjacency to stationery, cables, gaming, cameras and 3D printing
- Little dependence on water, heat or outdoor conditions

Stationery/craft scores equally, but desk organization ranks first because it supports more adjacent customer groups and a more neutral first brand position.

## 7. Base or Starter Kit?

### Recommendation: Starter Kit

An empty Base asks the customer to understand future platform value before receiving current value. The reviewed commercial systems reduce this problem through starter kits, bundles or a visible initial configuration.

The first offer should contain:

- One Base
- Catch-all tray module
- Pen/tool cup module
- Cable-routing module pack
- Phone-rest module

These accessory families recur across desk marketplaces and demonstrate the platform without requiring device-specific dimensions. This is a commercial assortment recommendation, not a structural design.

The Base should also be sold separately as an expansion SKU only after customers demonstrate capacity expansion intent.

No launch SKU requires water resistance, high heat, electronics or heavy-load claims.

## 9. Six-month Roadmap

### Month 1 - Commercial validation

- Compare Starter Kit and single-product offers.
- Interview users who already own three or more desk accessories.
- Test price, color-set and expansion interest.
- Define kill criteria before product development.

### Month 2 - Core release wave

- Release or pilot SKUs 1-6 only after the Starter Kit offer passes.
- Track kit conversion, module attach rate, print yield, packing time and returns.

### Month 3 - Core expansion

- Add SKUs 7-10.
- Measure second-order behavior rather than total order count alone.

### Month 4 - Creator and craft tests

- Test camera-battery/card and craft-tool modules with owners of actual equipment.
- Keep test modules out of the permanent catalog until repeat intent appears.

### Month 5 - Gaming and display tests

- Test controller/headphone and lightweight collectible modules.
- Avoid licensed character dependence and unverified fit claims.

### Month 6 - Portfolio gate

- Expand the winning vertical to SKUs 19-24.
- Remove low-attach or redundant modules.
- Decide whether PETG procurement is justified by lost demand, not by engineering preference.

The detailed release gates are in `sku-roadmap.csv`.

## 10. SKU Expansion Map

The platform can support a 100-SKU digital catalog within three years if expansion is gated by attach rate and repeat orders:

- Core platform: 10
- General desk: 15
- Stationery/craft: 15
- Gaming: 15
- Collectible/hobby display: 15
- Camera/creator: 10
- 3D-printer maintenance: 10
- Dry vanity: 5
- Dry coffee: 5

Total capacity: 100 SKUs.

This is not a promise to launch 100 physical-stock items. On-demand production and digital inventory are essential; otherwise color and long-tail fit variants would create excessive operating complexity.

## 11. PETG Expansion Path

PETG should be introduced only after PLA sales reveal demand outside the original boundary.

Potential later areas:

- Humid-room modules that do not receive continuous water
- Flexible or snap-intensive accessories
- Higher-impact under-desk and camera accessories
- Light workshop organization
- Coffee-zone organizers closer to moisture but away from heat and food contact

Still excluded without additional materials and validation:

- Automotive interiors
- Outdoor products
- Safety-critical or high-load products
- Direct hot-surface use
- Continuous wet use

## 12. Business Risks

1. Customers may prefer existing Gridfinity or SKADIS-compatible modules over a new Base.
2. A Starter Kit may become too expensive once printing, inspection and packing are included.
3. The catalog can grow faster than repeat demand.
4. Device-specific modules create fit support and obsolete inventory.
5. PLA can creep or fracture when customers exceed the intended boundary.
6. Color expansion creates raw-material and photography complexity.
7. Small follow-up orders may not absorb shipping cost.
8. Easy-to-print accessories are easy to copy.
9. Community files may have licenses that prohibit commercial use.
10. Marketplace popularity may represent file downloads rather than finished-product demand.

## 13. Required MVP Metrics

Before calling the product a platform, measure:

- Starter Kit conversion rate
- Base-to-accessory attach rate
- Average number of modules in the first order
- 30/90/180-day second-order rate
- Follow-up order contribution margin after shipping
- Print success rate by SKU and color
- Return rate by fit and breakage reason
- Percentage of revenue from the five best modules
- Customer request count for modules not yet offered

Platform success should be defined by second-order behavior, not the number of CAD files or SKUs.

## 14. Final Verdict

# GO WITH LIMITATIONS

### GO

- Start with a desktop micro-organization platform hypothesis.
- Use PLA only for the first MVP.
- Enter through a complete Starter Kit.
- Keep the first ten SKUs within dry, indoor and low-load use.
- Treat color and personalization as commercial variables.

### Limitations

- A proprietary Base has not yet beaten compatibility with existing standards.
- Exact print time, filament use and failure rate cannot be claimed before printable designs and slicing exist.
- PLA durability must be confirmed with physical load, creep and drop testing.
- No 100-SKU expansion should occur without repeat-order evidence.
