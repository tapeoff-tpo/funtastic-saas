# FUN-TASTIC Brand Validation Evidence Gaps

Checked: 2026-07-18

## Current Evidence Boundary

Phase 4-7 established internal strategy and external analogies. No FUN-TASTIC customer has yet been observed choosing, buying, assembling, expanding, reusing, or recommending this platform.

## Critical Gaps

### G1. Category comprehension

Unknown:

- Whether “connection” means a physical module system to customers
- Whether the English promise is understood in Korean-market use
- Whether the message is mistaken for electronics, smart home, or a generic lifestyle slogan

Required evidence:

- H1 randomized unaided message comprehension

### G2. Actual organizing behavior

Unknown:

- Which recent clutter events create purchase
- Which purchased organizers remain in use
- Why customers add, abandon, or replace organizing products

Required evidence:

- Non-leading interviews beginning with recent behavior
- Existing purchase or usage proof where consent permits

### G3. Proprietary-standard trust

Unknown:

- Whether compatibility feels valuable or restrictive
- What proof makes a long-term promise credible
- Whether service, pricing, breadth, or discontinuation is the largest concern

Required evidence:

- H2 and H7 interviews and promise/control test

### G4. Starter Kit versus simpler offers

Unknown:

- Whether Base Only has any day-one value
- Whether Desk Starter clarifies the platform or feels like a forced bundle
- Whether Universal Starter messaging helps or creates unsafe all-environment assumptions

Required evidence:

- H3 randomized monadic offer test

### G5. Follow-up module behavior

Unknown:

- Which module is selected next
- Whether the trigger is function, capacity, Zone, identity, or service
- Whether shipping and proprietary pricing make follow-up orders irrational

Required evidence:

- Concrete next-module choice
- Later paid or deposit-backed behavior
- Actual post-pilot second order

### G6. Cross-Zone value

Unknown:

- Whether customers want the same interface in another Zone
- Whether modules are moved or another Base is purchased
- Whether aesthetic and material expectations change by Zone

Required evidence:

- H5 scenario test
- Later observation with more than one suitable Zone

### G7. Physical assembly value

Unknown:

- Whether guided slide, hook-and-slide, or short-turn bayonet is understood
- Whether click satisfaction outweighs added effort
- One-hand use, force consistency, wear, fear, rattle, and partial engagement

Required evidence:

- Physical Top 3 interface prototypes and usability testing

Text, diagrams, and interview intentions cannot close this gap.

### G8. Color and personalization value

Unknown:

- Whether pastel or vivid color changes choice
- Whether personalization carries a premium
- Whether aesthetic praise survives a functional trade-off

Required evidence:

- H6 controlled collection task
- Later price and purchase test with real CMF samples

### G9. Pricing and unit economics

Unknown:

- Actual production, labor, packaging, fulfillment, return, and support cost
- Acceptable Starter Kit and follow-up module price
- Shipping threshold for a small second order

Required evidence:

- Cost model before price stimulus
- Comparable-market evidence
- Randomized or structured price research
- Paid behavior

No price should be invented for the current instruments.

### G10. Brand assets and legal clearance

Unknown:

- Trademark availability for FUN-TASTIC names and marks
- Final Korean and English verbal identity
- Legally safe interface and visual differentiation
- Rights for future collaborations

Required evidence:

- Legal and trademark review
- Naming and comprehension tests
- IP review after interface direction exists

### G11. Operational compatibility promise

Unknown:

- Who owns a ten-year commitment
- Adapter and migration policy
- Service-part availability
- Funding and catalog support for discontinued modules

Required evidence:

- Approved operational policy and owner
- Versioning and migration process
- Legal review of public language

Until then, test a migration commitment rather than a ten-year guarantee.

### G12. Sample and research operations

Unknown:

- Recruitment channels
- Incentive
- Segment incidence
- Baseline comprehension and variance
- Attrition and data-quality rate
- Minimum commercially meaningful effect

Required evidence:

- Pilot
- Power analysis
- Predeclared exclusion and analysis plan

## Highest-Priority Sequence

1. Recruit and run instrument pilot
2. Correct message and offer-card defects
3. Freeze primary outcomes and calculate decision sample
4. Run message and Starter Kit decision tests
5. Run collection choice and promise subtests
6. Build physical interface test pieces for H4 in a later phase
7. Use actual cost inputs before price testing
8. Test paid Starter Kit and follow-up behavior

## Current Conclusion

The research system is `ready-for-customer-test`, but every customer-facing hypothesis remains unverified.
