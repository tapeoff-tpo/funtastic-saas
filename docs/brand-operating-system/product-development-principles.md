# FUN-TASTIC Product Development Principles

Checked: 2026-07-18

## Product Thesis

An approved FUN-TASTIC product is not merely printable or attractive. It is a useful platform module whose interaction, compatibility, catalog role, service plan, and brand behavior are clear.

## Before Development

The proposal must define:

- Customer and observed job
- Current workaround or competing solution
- Why the platform improves the job
- Starter Kit, expansion, service, or collection role
- Intended Zone and environment class
- Existing module overlap
- Evidence gaps and test plan

No CAD or detailed design should begin when the only rationale is “this would look good in the collection.”

## Product Principles

### Solve one legible job

The first image and first sentence should communicate what changes for the customer.

### Participate in the platform

The interface, Base relationship, compatibility metadata, collection role, or service function must be genuine.

### Preserve familiar interaction

Newness should come from capability, arrangement, or expression, not from relearning attachment without reason.

### Create a next step

The product should expose one credible adjacent module, Base, Zone, or service path without making the current purchase feel incomplete.

### Minimize orphan risk

Avoid narrow fit variants until repeated evidence justifies support and long-term availability.

### Separate function from edition

Core utility remains available in evergreen form. Seasonal color or collaboration should not monopolize a necessary function.

### Design for lifecycle

Define service parts, cleaning, compatibility version, retirement state, and migration before launch.

### Respect environment boundaries

PLA-first dry-zone evidence does not authorize Bathroom, Kitchen-wet, heat, impact, or high-load claims.

### Earn complexity

Additional parts, colors, instructions, assembly, and packaging must create customer value greater than the support burden.

### Make the system easier to understand

Every launch should improve or preserve catalog clarity. If customers need internal terminology to choose correctly, the offer is not ready.

## Product Roles

Every SKU must have exactly one primary role:

- Starter: completes the first job
- Universal core: works across multiple Zones
- Zone core: essential within one Zone
- Object fit: serves a repeated specific fit need
- Expansion: increases Base capacity or configuration
- Identity: label, color, personalization, or display
- Service: replacement, adapter, cover, or repair
- Edition: bounded color, story, or collaboration layer

Secondary roles may be recorded, but the primary role controls approval and lifecycle metrics.

## Evidence Progression

1. Hypothesis
2. Customer or market evidence
3. Offer evidence
4. Prototype interaction evidence
5. Production and claims evidence
6. Paid launch evidence
7. Repeat and cross-Zone evidence
8. Evergreen or retirement decision

Do not promote a product because the team has invested time in it.

## Required Launch Record

- Product role
- Job statement
- Evidence references
- Compatibility version
- Base classes and Zones
- Environment and load class
- Naming and collection placement
- Package contents
- Next recommended module
- Service and replacement plan
- Claims test results
- Approval decision and owners
- Review date and retirement criteria
