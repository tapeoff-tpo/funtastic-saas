# Phase 9.2 Design Intent

## Status

`waiting-for-block-direction-approval`

This phase resets the product direction before engineering begins. It defines a visual and interaction promise, not a final mechanism.

## Why Phase 9 and 9.1 missed the intent

The earlier studies treated the interface as a load-bearing docking problem. Their plates, rails, hooks, and short-turn locks can compare engineering behavior, but they do not test the intended product experience: picking up a block, pressing it directly into another block or base, collecting more blocks, and rebuilding the arrangement.

Phase 9.1 improved the outer shape but retained a product-on-dock relationship. A rounded cover over a docking mechanism is not the same as a block platform.

## Locked product intent

The following requirements take priority over the previous interface studies:

1. Every module reads as an independent, hand-held block.
2. Primary assembly is a straight push into a visible receiving cell.
3. Primary removal is a straight hand pull from that cell.
4. The first interaction must be understandable without instructions.
5. Multiple blocks must form a coherent horizontal, 2x2, and layered system.
6. Functional modules must grow from the same block grammar rather than sit on unrelated adapters.
7. Color must create combinational value across connected blocks.
8. The form must remain independent from LEGO geometry and compatibility.

## This phase decides

- Block proportion and visual character
- How visibly the base participates in the product family
- Where the push-in relationship is perceived
- How horizontal and vertical expansion should read
- Which product-family expression is strong enough to enter CAD development

## This phase does not decide

- Internal retention geometry
- Clearances, detent force, wear life, or load rating
- Final dimensions
- Print orientation or slicing settings
- Final accessory assortment
- Production approval

The renders are design-direction images. They are not proof of PLA durability, manufacturability, or final fit.
