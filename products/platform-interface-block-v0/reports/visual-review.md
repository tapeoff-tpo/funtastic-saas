# Block-Form Universal Interface V0 - Visual Review

Status: `pass-for-physical-prototype`

The first render pass failed because it was too dark and the exploded Module
showed its exterior rather than the mating face. Lighting and exploded layout
were corrected before this review.

## Acceptance Criteria

| Criterion | Result | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Reads as a block family, not flat coupons | Pass | 16 mm hand-grippable Modules sit on a consistent 3 mm Base reveal |
| Assembled proportion can support a living-platform product | Pass with limitation | 44 x 44 x 23 mm assembly reads as a compact docked block; final accessory function is intentionally absent |
| 2x2 arrangement communicates expansion | Pass | `renders/block-grid-mockup.png` shows one pitch and shared visual grammar |
| Interface does not dominate the exterior | Pass | Rails, hooks and bayonet tracks are hidden after assembly |
| A/B/C motion differences are visible | Pass | Exploded view exposes continuous rails, two hooks and three arc tracks |

## Phase 9 Correction

Phase 9 over-prioritized equal flat test coupons. It could compare mechanics but
could not test grip, block recognition, assembled coherence or array behavior.
Phase 9.1 retains the three mechanisms while replacing the visible object with
a common rounded block family and adding assembled, grip and grid views.

## Remaining Visual Limitations

- The Module is a neutral proportional block, not a Tray, Hook or finished SKU.
- Rendered colors identify variants and are not a final CMF decision.
- Neighbor removal and hand clearance require the printed 2x2 test, not imagery.
