# LEGO-Feel Definition

## Meaning

“LEGO-feel” describes a behavior loop, not an appearance:

1. **Pick** a complete block up with one hand.
2. **Aim** it at an obvious receiving cell.
3. **Press** it in with one straight motion.
4. **Feel** an unmistakable seated state.
5. **Pull** it out and reconfigure the system.

The reward comes from immediate comprehension, tactile completion, reversibility, visible accumulation, and the usefulness of each new combination.

## Visual signals

- A block has a graspable perimeter and enough body depth to feel independent.
- The receiving cell is visible before assembly but visually quiet after assembly.
- A small consistent reveal separates adjacent blocks.
- Repeated block rhythm makes another addition feel natural.
- Functional geometry is integrated into the top or face of the block.
- Matte color and controlled FDM texture reinforce a coherent collection.

## Completion cues for later engineering

The approved direction should later test tactile force rise, a short seating event, a clear final stop, visible flushness, and stable removal force. Sound may support completion, but it must not be the only cue.

## IP boundary

The platform must not use LEGO logos, cylindrical stud fields, LEGO brick proportions, exact dimensions, underside tube systems, minifigure language, or interoperability claims. The direction studies instead use rounded cells, clipped corners, or frame-and-core forms with a distinct living-product character.

## Non-negotiable rejection cases

- A module must slide along a rail.
- A module must hook and then translate.
- A module must rotate to lock.
- The interface looks like a thin plate with a product placed on top.
- Expansion is only illustrated rather than inherent in the repeated block grammar.
- The form could be mistaken for a LEGO-compatible product.
