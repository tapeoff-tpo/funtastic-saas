# FUN-TASTIC Brand Expansion Strategy

Checked: 2026-07-18

## Expansion Model

Expand through customer evidence in this order:

`first job -> adjacent module -> deeper routine -> second Zone -> new Base class -> new material -> partner ecosystem`

Skipping a step increases the chance that the brand becomes a catalog of unrelated objects.

## Four Expansion Directions

### Depth

Add capability within a proven routine.

- Preferred when customers already own the Base
- Measured through attach rate and follow-up orders
- Example category: another Study or Work module

### Bridge

Move a familiar module or interaction into an adjacent Zone.

- Preferred when reuse is credible
- Measured through cross-Zone adoption and second-Base behavior
- Example category: Work to Bedroom or Beauty

### Expression

Add personalization, color, seasonal story, or collaboration.

- Approved only on proven products
- Measured separately from functional module growth
- Cannot carry necessary function exclusively

### Infrastructure

Add a Base class, material, adapter, service part, catalog tool, or partner tier.

- Often less visible but essential for ten-year trust
- Requires compatibility and lifecycle ownership
- Must precede growth that depends on it

## Expansion Priority

1. Service and compatibility gaps
2. Repeated customer job gaps
3. Starter Kit and core assortment gaps
4. Proven adjacent Zone bridges
5. Personalization and controlled palettes
6. Collaboration
7. Experimental novelty

This order prevents campaigns from consuming capacity needed to keep the platform reliable.

## Portfolio Balance

At every scale, management should separately track:

- Functional modules
- Base and interface infrastructure
- Service parts
- Color-only variants
- Personalization
- Editions and collaborations
- Experimental and partner modules

No single ratio is asserted before sales evidence exists. The purpose is to stop decorative SKU growth from being mistaken for platform depth.

## Expansion Review

Each proposal must state:

- Expansion direction: depth, bridge, expression, or infrastructure
- Existing customer behavior that supports it
- Prior modules or Bases made more valuable
- New support and lifecycle burden
- Collection and naming placement
- Compatibility and environment implications
- Evidence required to continue
- Kill, archive, or migration path

## Stop Rules

Pause expansion when:

- Customers cannot identify the correct module or compatibility generation.
- Follow-up purchases are mainly replacements caused by failure.
- Active usage grows more slowly than catalog size.
- New Zones do not reuse the interface or module vocabulary.
- Collaborations attract attention but no core-platform adoption.
- Service parts, documentation, or support fall behind releases.
- Color and fit proliferation damage fulfillment or discoverability.

## Relationship to the Roadmap

`long-term-brand-roadmap.md` defines the governance required at 100, 500, 1000, and 5000 logical SKUs. Expansion should stop at the highest stage whose operating systems are actually in place.
