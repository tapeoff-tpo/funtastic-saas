# Interface Rationale

## Architecture translation

The 2x2 Base owns four independent sockets. Every block uses one asymmetric five-sided Facet footprint, enters vertically, stops against the Base top, and leaves vertically. No lateral slide, hook sequence, rotational lock, module-to-module connection, or module stacking is present.

## Separated interface duties

- **Guide:** broad five-sided guide and socket walls align the block and carry lateral load.
- **Load surfaces:** guide walls resist horizontal movement; the module shoulder and Base top define vertical seating.
- **Retention:** two short shallow side beads meet hidden pockets near the end of travel.
- **Stop:** the broad underside shoulder contacts the Base top; the guide bottom retains 0.4mm terminal clearance.
- **Orientation:** the clipped corner prevents ambiguous rotation and carries the Facet Totem family cue.

Retention does not carry the complete structural load. It is deliberately short and shallow to minimize repeated PLA deformation.

## Fit comparison

Light, Standard, and Firm preserve exactly the same external block, insertion path, stop, and load surfaces. Only guide side clearance and hidden pocket relief change. The common Base is identical for all three.

## V0 limitations

- CAD interference is not a force prediction.
- P2S dimensional variation, filament moisture, color, cooling, and first-layer behavior may change fit.
- Shallow bead wear and Base-wall whitening require physical cycle testing.
- The geometry is an independent Facet footprint, but commercial use still requires IP/FTO review.
