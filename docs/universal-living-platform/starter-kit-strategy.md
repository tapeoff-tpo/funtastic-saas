# FUN-TASTIC Universal Living Platform - Starter Kit Strategy

Checked: 2026-07-18

## Entry Principle

Sell a complete first job, not an empty Base and not a box of unrelated accessories. Every Starter Kit should provide immediate utility, demonstrate reconfiguration, and leave at least one obvious expansion path.

The kits below are assortment hypotheses. They are not approved launch SKUs, product designs, or demand forecasts.

## Recommended Launch Sequence

1. Study Starter
2. Work Starter
3. Bedroom or Beauty bridge kit, selected by paid demand
4. Living Room or Kids bridge kit, selected by module reuse evidence
5. Conditional Coffee, Entryway, and Pet pilots
6. Travel after a portable Base is independently validated
7. Bathroom, Kitchen-wet, and Laundry-humid kits only after material expansion

Study and Work should share most module families. That makes the second kit an ecosystem extension rather than a separate product line.

## Follow-up Purchase Logic

Follow-up modules should be triggered by observable events:

- Capacity expansion: the first Base is full.
- New object: the user adds a device, tool, hobby, pet, or routine.
- New Zone: a known module job is repeated elsewhere.
- Better fit: a generic tray is replaced with an object-specific insert.
- Identity: the user adds labels, names, colors, or a collaboration edition.
- Service: a retention part, cover, or adapter is replaced without discarding the module.

Do not count color variants alone as proof of platform repeat purchase.

## Bundle Rules

- Every kit must solve one visible job on day one.
- At least three module families should also work in another Zone.
- A kit should not require customers to understand interface terminology.
- The package should show the installed configuration and two legitimate reconfigurations.
- Wet-zone or load claims must not migrate from one Base class to another.
- Base-only sales should be positioned as expansion after Starter Kit adoption, not as the primary entry offer.

## Evidence Gap

No current source provides FUN-TASTIC conversion, module attach rate, cross-Zone adoption, or repeat-order sequences. The recommended bundles therefore require offer testing before product development.
