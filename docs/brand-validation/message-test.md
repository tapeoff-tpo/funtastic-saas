# FUN-TASTIC Brand Message Test

Status: designed; no participants or results

## Objective

Identify which message best communicates an expandable living platform while remaining differentiated, credible, and interesting.

Primary hypothesis: H1

Secondary hypotheses: H2, H5, H7

## Message Conditions

### A

> One familiar connection, wherever life happens.

### B

> 하나의 연결로 생활의 모든 공간을 정리하다.

### C

> 필요한 만큼 연결하고, 생활에 맞게 확장하다.

### D

> 정리가 아니라 나만의 생활 시스템.

Do not add a subtitle, logo, product image, or color cue during primary comprehension measurement.

## Experimental Design

1. Randomly assign one condition.
2. Show the message for three seconds on the same neutral card.
3. Remove it.
4. Ask unaided understanding and expected-product questions.
5. Record the response before showing any explanation.
6. Collect understanding, differentiation, trust, and curiosity ratings.
7. Ask which phrase was unclear.
8. Only after primary measures, show the neutral platform explanation card.
9. Optional secondary comparison may show all four messages in randomized order.

The same participant's comparative ranking is secondary because prior exposure changes interpretation.

## Primary Measures

### Unaided comprehension

Code the first complete response:

- `platform-correct`: Base or common connection plus add-on or rearrangeable modules
- `platform-partial`: understands expansion or modules but not both
- `organizer-only`: expects a single organizer or storage product
- `electronic-connection`: expects smart-home, app, network, or device connectivity
- `single-base-everywhere`: expects one identical physical Base in all environments
- `abstract-lifestyle`: understands only a general lifestyle philosophy
- `unclear`

### Rating measures

- Understanding
- Differentiation
- Trust
- Purchase curiosity

Use a 1-5 scale with randomized item order.

### Diagnostic measures

- Product or service expected
- Target customer expected
- Unclear word or phrase
- Wet-room, electronic, or LEGO-like interpretation

## Proposed Decision Rules

These are research proposals, not established thresholds.

### Candidate winner

- Reaches at least 70% `platform-correct` in the powered decision test
- `electronic-connection` plus `single-base-everywhere` misconceptions stay at or below 20%
- Median trust is at least 4 on the 5-point scale
- Does not win curiosity while materially losing comprehension or trust
- No priority segment shows a severe unexplained reversal

### Revision required

- Correct comprehension is 50-69%
- Differentiation is high but organizer-only or abstract interpretation remains common
- Trust is below the proposed threshold but the credibility problem is diagnosable

### Failure

- No message reaches 50% correct comprehension after one controlled wording revision
- The leading interpretation is electronic connectivity or one identical Base in every Zone
- A message is remembered but the product category remains unclear
- Curiosity depends mainly on ambiguity

## Automatic Stop Conditions

- Stop public use of “all spaces” language if participants infer wet, hot, high-load, or safety suitability.
- Stop an English-only message as primary Korean copy if comprehension failure is language-driven.
- Stop any message that participants repeatedly associate with LEGO compatibility or direct imitation.
- Do not declare a winner from ratings without unaided comprehension.

## Compatibility Promise Subtest

Test H7 separately after the primary brand-message response.

### Control

> 같은 연결 방식으로 필요한 모듈을 추가할 수 있습니다.

### Promise

> 연결 규격이 바뀌더라도 기존 제품을 계속 사용할 수 있도록 호환 또는 전환 방법을 안내합니다.

The Promise avoids an unapproved “ten-year guarantee.” Measure:

- Trust
- Lock-in concern
- Follow-up purchase interest
- Required proof
- Expected company obligation

Do not use “10-year compatibility” publicly until operations, legal review, migration policy, and service ownership exist.
